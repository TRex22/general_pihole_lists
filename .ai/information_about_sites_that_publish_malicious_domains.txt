The emerging IETF standard for defanging is: replace http/https with hxxp/hxxps, replace every . with [.], and replace @ with [@]. IETF Not everyone follows this yet though.

Practical tip for your scraping workflow
Since you're already parsing THN, here's the defanging hierarchy to handle programmatically when you scrape other sources:
pythonimport re

def refang(domain):
    domain = domain.replace('[.]', '.')
    domain = domain.replace('(.)','.')
    domain = domain.replace('[DOT]', '.').replace('[dot]', '.')
    domain = re.sub(r'\s+', '', domain)  # strip spaces
    return domain
The SANS ISC and Cisco Talos blogs are probably your highest-value additions alongside THN — they're consistent, high-volume, and reliably defang with [.]. Unit 42 is excellent for more detailed campaign reports with larger IOC appendices.
